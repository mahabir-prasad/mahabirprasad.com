## Course code: CHM23CO11

## Course Learning Outcome

Students will:

- Comprehend the characteristics of the gaseous state and explore its diverse properties, including the principles governing ideal gas behavior.
- Realize the characteristics and diverse properties of substances in their liquid state.
- Gain insight into the characteristics and behaviors of solid states and their diverse properties.
- Investigate the process of electrolyte hydrolysis in aqueous solutions.
- Determine the reaction kinetics and its applications.

## Syllabus

- **Kinetic theory of gasses:**
Kinetic molecular model of a gas: postulates and
derivation of the kinetic gas equation; collision frequency;
collision diameter; mean free path and viscosity of gasses,
calculation of ${\sigma}$ from ${\eta}$; Maxwell distribution and its use
in evaluating molecular velocities and average kinetic
energy, law of equipartition of energy, degrees of freedom
and molecular basis of heat capacities.
- **Behavior of real gasses:**
Deviations from ideal gas behavior, compressibility
factor, Z, and its variation with pressure for different
gasses. Causes of deviation from ideal behaviour. Van der
Waals equation of state, its derivation and application in
explaining real gas behaviour, Virial equation of state;
Van der waals equation expressed in virial form and
calculation of Boyle temperature.
- **Liquid state and colligative properties of solutions:**
Physical properties of liquids; vapor pressure, surface
tension and coefficient of viscosity, and their
determination. Effect of addition of various solutes on
surface tension and viscosity. Temperature variation of
viscosity of liquids and comparison with that of gasses.
Raoult's and Henry's Laws and their applications.
Calculations of various colligative properties [(i) relative
lowering of vapour pressure, (ii) elevation of boiling
point, (iii) Depression of freezing point, (iv) osmotic
pressure].

Practical: Determination of viscosity of a liquid using Ostwald viscometer.

- **Solid state and Crystallography:**
Nature of the solid state, law of constancy of interfacial
angles, law of rational indices, Miller indices, elementary
ideas of symmetry, symmetry elements and symmetry
operations, qualitative idea of point and space groups,
seven crystal systems and fourteen Bravais lattices.
X-ray diffraction, Bragg's law, crystal structure of NaCl,
CsCl. Defects in crystals. Glasses and liquid crystals

Practical: Determination of melting point of solids using melting point apparatus.

- **Introduction to kinetics:**
Order and molecularity of a reaction, differential and
integrated form of rate expressions up to second order
reactions, experimental methods of the determination of
rate laws, Temperature dependence of reaction rates;
Arrhenius equation; activation energy.

Practical: Determination of surface tension of a liquid using a stalagmometer.
